CEYLON HERBAL TEAS

RANAWARA TEA

Ranawara or Cassia auriculata is a popular herb among Sri Lankans. Ranwara plants are mostly available in the coastal line and the dry zones in Sri Lanka. The herbal tea made with the flower of this plant has been widely used by Sri Lankans for generations. It is prepared by boiling Ranawara flowers in hot water. After it is left in boiling water for 20 - 30 minutes, the tea can be strained while the residue can be discarded. Now, Ranawara tea is available in tea bags making it accessible and available to many around the world. The regular consumption of Ranawara is recommended due to its multiple benefits.

MORINGA TEA

The leaves of Moringa, the drumstick tree, are boiled to make moringa herbal tea, a refreshing healthy beverage that supports the body in many ways. The moringa tree is also known as the “Miracle Tree” because of its amazing values. Moringa Tea is made by steeping dried moringa leaves in freshly boiled spring water for about five minutes before being strained.

HIBISCUS TEA

Hibiscus is a flowering plant that belongs to the mallow family. There are many species of the plant that grow in all locations and climates. Even though it looks like an ordinary garden plant, it comprises a lot of health benefits. The herbal tea of hibiscus is one good way to experience these benefits. It is made by steeping hibiscus flowers in boiling water for five minutes. It has a tart taste. Therefore, it is often sweetened using lime or honey.
